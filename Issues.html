Stand Against Plastic Waste Reduction Regulations

People toss away heavy-duty reusable bags at a greater rate once plastic bags are banned. Part of the issue is that, despite the world-saving hyperbole that generally promotes and supports plastic bag bans, plastic bags aren't all that bad. According to the EPA's national data from 2013, single-use plastic shopping bags accounted for 0.28 percent of total municipal solid trash generated in the United States. A more detailed litter study in Fort Worth, Texas (published in the Austin assessment) revealed that single-use bags account for just 0.12% of the weight of trash in the city (which does not have a ban). ​Residents would begin to regard reusable bags as though they were disposable bags. According to the evaluation, the number of reusable plastic bags presently arriving at the city's recycling facilities is "almost comparable to the quantity of all single-use bags removed from the recycling stream as a result of the legislation imposed in 2013."
